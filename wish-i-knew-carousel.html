Wish I Knew This Sooner
Take CS 205 (Data Structures) the semester right after CS 176.
Almost every upper-level CS course needs it as a prerequisite. If you delay it, you'll be locked out of electives you actually want — like AI, Game Dev, or Cybersecurity.
— S.O.S Peer Mentor, CSSE Major

S.O.S Peer Mentors

02
🔬
Wish I Knew This Sooner
Start asking professors about research early — even as a sophomore.
The School of Science pushes student-faculty research hard. Bio and Chem labs are always looking for undergrads. You don't need to be a genius — just show up and be curious. It looks incredible on grad school apps.
— S.O.S Peer Mentor, Biology Major

S.O.S Peer Mentors

03
🧮
Wish I Knew This Sooner
Don't sleep on the Math tutoring center — even if you think you're "fine."
Calc II and Linear Algebra catch a lot of people off guard. The tutoring center is free, and the math peer tutors have literally taken the same classes with the same professors. Use them.
— S.O.S Peer Mentor, Math Major

S.O.S Peer Mentors

04
💼
Wish I Knew This Sooner
Check Handshake for campus jobs and internships — not just Google.
Monmouth posts campus lab assistant jobs, IT help desk positions, and paid research gigs on Handshake that you won't find anywhere else. Set up alerts for "School of Science" and check weekly.
— S.O.S Peer Mentor, Chemistry Major

S.O.S Peer Mentors

05
🎯
Wish I Knew This Sooner
Pick your gen-eds strategically — they can double-count.
Some gen-ed courses satisfy multiple requirements at once. Talk to your advisor before registering randomly. One smart pick can save you an entire semester's worth of credits.
— S.O.S Peer Mentor, CSSE Major

S.O.S Peer Mentors

06
🤝
Wish I Knew This Sooner
Join at least one club outside your major. Seriously.
It's easy to get tunnel vision in science. But some of the best connections (and stress relief) come from clubs you'd never expect. Plus, employers and grad schools love seeing range.
— S.O.S Peer Mentor, Biology Major
